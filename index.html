Cartographies of the Non‑Dual Path

Vedanta · Maps · The Unnameable

Vedanta (from the Sanskrit veda – knowledge, anta – end) is the philosophical culmination of the ancient Upanishads. It teaches that the individual self (Atman) is ultimately identical with the absolute reality (Brahman). This is non‑duality (Advaita): not one‑ness as a number, but the recognition that separation was never real.

Why “cartography”? Because every spiritual tradition, every realized master, every scripture is a map – not the territory itself. The territory is the Self, the Absolute, the Tao. It cannot be drawn, described, or captured. But a good map, drawn from authentic experience, can orient the seeker and reveal the pitfalls of the journey. The three cartographies presented here — Advaita Vedanta, The Vedantic Messiah, and Taoism · The Unnamed Way – are not competitors. They are different paths up the same mountain, each with its own language, its own silences, and its own unique precision.

These pages are not scholarly dissertations. They are contemplative tools, compiled from primary sources (interviews, recorded talks, authorised transcripts) and arranged as practical guides. Sanskrit and Chinese terms have tooltip definitions on hover. The goal is not to “believe” but to see – to verify for yourself whether the pointer is true. As Ramana Maharshi said: “The Self is the only Guru. All outer gurus merely point back to that inner Guru.”

Nisargadatta Maharaj said: “Give up all questions except one: 'Who am I?' After all, the only fact you are sure of is that you are. The 'I am' is certain. The 'I am this' is not.” — I Am That
