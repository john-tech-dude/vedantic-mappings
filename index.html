Complete maps from mistaken identity to liberation, grounded in the Vedantic tradition

I

Ramana Maharshi

The Sage of Arunachala

A comprehensive cartography of Self-enquiry (Atma Vichara) — from the 1896 death experience that became liberation, through the seven stages of realization, to the practice of abiding as the Self. Grounded in the Upanishadic tradition and the Advaita Vedanta of Adi Shankaracharya.

1879 – 1950 Tiruvannamalai Advaita Vedanta
II

Nisargadatta Maharaj

The Inchegiri Sampradaya

A complete mapping of the radical teaching on the distinction between consciousness and pure awareness. From the starting condition of mistaken identity through the seven stages to the Absolute (Parabrahman) — prior to the "I Am" itself. Rooted in the Navnath lineage and the teachings of Sri Siddharameshwar Maharaj.

1897 – 1981 Mumbai Inchegiri Sampradaya
III

The Vedantic Messiah

Gospel & Upanishad

A contemplative exploration of the Gospel of Jesus and the non-dual wisdom of the Upanishads. Five pages mapping the resonances between Christian mysticism and Advaita Vedanta — from the Kingdom within to the art of holy negation. Not a merger, but an illumination of shadows.

Contemplative Comparative Christian · Vedantic
